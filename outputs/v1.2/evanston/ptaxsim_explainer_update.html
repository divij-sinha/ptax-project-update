PTAXSIM Tax Explainer

What are property taxes?

Property tax is owed by property owners annually, for both residential and commercial properties.

These liabilities are decided per Property Parcel, and each parcel is uniquely identifiable with a 14-digit Property Index Number or PIN

Every PIN belongs to multiple tax districts (local government bodies), that need funds for their purposes.

The Cook County Assessor’s Office, the State, and county taxing districts (schools, parks, etc.) work together to calculate fair property taxes for residents.

The property taxes are collected centrally and then distributed to each district

Breaking the bill down - Your local school district!

One of these districts is your local school district - Evanston Township High School 202! A portion of your taxes is sent to the agency to ensure it can continue operations.

Generally, these districts fund the local elementary, middle and high schools, along with community colleges in the area.

But where can Evanston Township High School 202 raise the money from? From all the properties within the district boundaries!
The total taxable value of all properties falling in Evanston Township High School 202 make up its tax base. For 2023, this was $4.22b

Tax District Agency Tax Base Agency Tax Levy Agency Tax Rate Final Tax Owed

The cost of services of Evanston Township High School 202 is therefore divided amongst all the property owners, and Evanston Township High School 202 sets its agency tax rate equal to:

\[ \mathrm{{Levy \over Base} = Tax \hspace{0.1cm} rate} \]

Using real numbers, the tax rate equals: \[ \mathrm{\$89.03M \over \$4.22B} = 2.11\% \]

As the owner of Parcel (10-13-204-006-0000), in the year 2023 your property is worth $350k, of that, your taxable amount (or addition to the tax base) is $105.57k1.

The previously calculated Rate is then applied to your Addition To The Tax Base:

\[ (\$105.57K * 2.11\%) = \$2.23K \]

TIFs!

TIFs or Tax Increment Financing districts are special vehicles that redirect your funding towards other purposes

Your parcel - Parcel (10-13-204-006-0000) - belongs to the following TIFs -

TIF Name Share of TIF
Evanston - Area 9 Five Fifths 33.95%

The way TIFs work is by funnelling a share of all your (post-exemption) taxes to the special purpose of the TIF.

The share of the TIF differs every year. It works by freezing the tax base of the Municipality to the year that the TIF was enacted, and any increase in the Tax Base is redirected to the TIF.

This means that for County Of Cook, the following amount is siphoned off to the TIF

\[ \$407.50 * 33.95\% = \$138.33 \]
